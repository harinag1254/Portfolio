/ My Process

How I Approach
Every Project

12+ years of UX practice distilled into five repeatable steps — from understanding the real problem to shipping designs that move measurable KPIs.

01 Discover & Research User Research

I start every project by understanding the real problem — not just the brief. Through stakeholder interviews, contextual observation, and data analysis I surface hidden pain points that shape everything downstream. No assumptions, only evidence.

User InterviewsContextual InquirySurveysHeuristic AuditAnalytics ReviewFigJamDovetail
02 Define & Synthesise IA · Personas

Raw research becomes design direction. I build personas grounded in real behaviour, map journey flows to expose friction, and restructure information architecture around how people actually think — not how the system is built.

PersonasJourney MappingAffinity MappingHMW StatementsInformation ArchitectureMiro
03 Design & Prototype UI · Figma

From paper sketches to high-fidelity interactive prototypes — I move fast and test often. AI-assisted tools (Figma Make, Claude AI) compress iteration time by ~25%, building scalable component systems that developers can implement without ambiguity.

WireframesHi-Fi PrototypesFigmaFigma MakeClaude AIDesign SystemsComponent Libraries
04 Test & Validate Usability · A/B

Design without testing is guesswork. I run moderated usability sessions and A/B tests against real tasks and KPIs — task success rate, time-on-task, drop-off rate — then feed findings directly back into the prototype in rapid cycles.

Usability TestingA/B TestingMazeTask Success RateDrop-off AnalysisHeatmaps
05 Deliver & Measure Handoff · KPIs

I work closely with developers in Agile sprints to ensure pixel-perfect delivery — structured Zeplin/Figma handoff, annotated specs, and component documentation. Post-launch, I track KPIs to close the feedback loop and identify next improvements.

Dev HandoffZeplinFigma SpecsAgile SprintsKPI TrackingJira
